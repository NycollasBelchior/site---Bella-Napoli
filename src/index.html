Our Story

A little piece of Naples, three generations deep

In 1994, Nonna Rosa Cuccia left the sun-warmed alleys of Naples with a suitcase of recipes, a jar of her mother's starter dough, and a belief that a meal made slowly is a meal made with love. She opened a six-table trattoria on a quiet stretch of King Street West, and Toronto has been finding its way to her door ever since.

Today, Bella Napoli is run by her son Marco and granddaughter Giulia, who still bake every pizza in a hand-built, wood-fired oven imported stone by stone from Campania. The pasta is rolled fresh each morning, the tomatoes come from San Marzano, and the tiramisu is still Nonna Rosa's own recipe — unchanged, unhurried, and utterly unforgettable.
